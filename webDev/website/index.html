Philosophy

Why This Matters

Technology is most interesting when it becomes tangible — when it connects software to the physical world, makes invisible systems visible, and shapes how people experience infrastructure and place.

The most fulfilling projects are often not the most efficient career moves, but the ones that balance technical depth with aesthetics, feel handcrafted rather than optimised, and remind me why I fell in love with engineering in the first place.

"Most of my ideas start while walking through cities. Side projects matter because they preserve creativity, reduce burnout, and encourage experimentation."

  1. Problem Definition — Understand requirements, constraints, and stakeholder needs
  2. System Design — Architecture, hardware selection, protocol decisions
  3. Prototyping — Build proof-of-concept, test assumptions
  4. Development — Implement core functionality with rigorous testing
  5. Integration — Hardware-software integration, sensor calibration
  6. Testing & Validation — Field testing, reliability verification
